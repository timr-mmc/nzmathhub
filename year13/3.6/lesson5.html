Equations of Tangent and Normal Lines

The derivative at a point gives us the gradient (slope) of the tangent line at that point. We can use this to find equations of tangents and normals.

Tangent Line

  • Touches the curve at one point
  • Has the same gradient as the curve
  • Gradient = $\frac{dy}{dx}$ at that point

Normal Line

  • Perpendicular to the tangent
  • Gradient = $\frac{-1}{\text{tangent gradient}}$
  • Forms a right angle with the curve

Standard Method

  1. Find the point: Substitute $x$ to find $y$-coordinate $(x_1, y_1)$
  2. Find gradient: Calculate $\frac{dy}{dx}$ and evaluate at $x_1$
  3. For tangent: Use $m = \frac{dy}{dx}|_{x=x_1}$
  4. For normal: Use $m = \frac{-1}{\frac{dy}{dx}|_{x=x_1}}$
  5. Line equation: Use $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$

Key Formula: Point-Gradient Form

$$y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$$

where $(x_1, y_1)$ is a point on the line and $m$ is the gradient

Worked Examples: Tangent Lines

Example 1: Basic Tangent

Question: Find the equation of the tangent to $y = x^2$ at $x = 2$
Step 1: Find the point ▼
$y = 2^2 = 4$, so point is $(2, 4)$
Step 2: Find gradient ▼
$\frac{dy}{dx} = 2x$
Step 3: Evaluate at $x = 2$ ▼
$m = 2(2) = 4$
Step 4: Use point-gradient form ▼
$y - 4 = 4(x - 2)$
✓ Answer: Simplify ▼
$y - 4 = 4x - 8$, so $y = 4x - 4$

Example 2: Tangent with Product Rule

Question: Find the tangent to $y = x(x + 1)$ at $x = 1$
Step 1: Find point ▼
$y = 1(1 + 1) = 2$, point is $(1, 2)$
Step 2: Differentiate ▼
$\frac{dy}{dx} = x \cdot 1 + (x + 1) \cdot 1 = 2x + 1$
Step 3: Evaluate ▼
$m = 2(1) + 1 = 3$
Step 4: Equation ▼
$y - 2 = 3(x - 1)$
✓ Answer: Simplify ▼
$y = 3x - 1$

Worked Examples: Normal Lines

Example 3: Basic Normal

Question: Find the normal to $y = x^2$ at $x = 2$
Step 1: Find point ▼
From Example 1, point is $(2, 4)$
Step 2: Tangent gradient ▼
From Example 1, $m_{\text{tangent}} = 4$
Step 3: Normal gradient ▼
$m_{\text{normal}} = \frac{-1}{4}$
Step 4: Equation ▼
$y - 4 = -\frac{1}{4}(x - 2)$
✓ Answer: Simplify ▼
$y = -\frac{1}{4}x + \frac{1}{2} + 4 = -\frac{1}{4}x + \frac{9}{2}$

Example 4: Normal with Chain Rule

Question: Find the normal to $y = (x + 1)^2$ at $x = 0$
Step 1: Find point ▼
$y = (0 + 1)^2 = 1$, point is $(0, 1)$
Step 2: Differentiate ▼
$\frac{dy}{dx} = 2(x + 1)$
Step 3: Tangent gradient ▼
$m_{\text{tangent}} = 2(0 + 1) = 2$
Step 4: Normal gradient ▼
$m_{\text{normal}} = -\frac{1}{2}$
✓ Answer: Equation ▼
$y - 1 = -\frac{1}{2}(x - 0)$, so $y = -\frac{1}{2}x + 1$

Practice: Tangent Lines - Minimal Change

Set 1: Same curve, different points

Curve: $y = x^2$, find tangent at different $x$-values

a) Tangent to $y = x^2$ at $x = 1$
Point: $(1, 1)$
$\frac{dy}{dx} = 2x$, at $x = 1$: $m = 2$
$y - 1 = 2(x - 1)$
$y = 2x - 1$
b) Tangent to $y = x^2$ at $x = 3$
Point: $(3, 9)$
$\frac{dy}{dx} = 2x$, at $x = 3$: $m = 6$
$y - 9 = 6(x - 3)$
$y = 6x - 9$
c) Tangent to $y = x^2$ at $x = -1$
Point: $(-1, 1)$
$\frac{dy}{dx} = 2x$, at $x = -1$: $m = -2$
$y - 1 = -2(x + 1)$
$y = -2x - 1$

Set 2: Varying the power

Similar curves with different powers, all at $x = 2$

a) Tangent to $y = x^2$ at $x = 2$
Point: $(2, 4)$
$\frac{dy}{dx} = 2x$, at $x = 2$: $m = 4$
$y = 4x - 4$
b) Tangent to $y = x^3$ at $x = 2$
Point: $(2, 8)$
$\frac{dy}{dx} = 3x^2$, at $x = 2$: $m = 12$
$y - 8 = 12(x - 2)$
$y = 12x - 16$
c) Tangent to $y = x^4$ at $x = 2$
Point: $(2, 16)$
$\frac{dy}{dx} = 4x^3$, at $x = 2$: $m = 32$
$y - 16 = 32(x - 2)$
$y = 32x - 48$

Practice: Normal Lines - Minimal Change

Set 3: From tangents to normals

We found tangents in Set 1, now find the normals

a) Normal to $y = x^2$ at $x = 1$
Point: $(1, 1)$, tangent gradient: $m = 2$
Normal gradient: $m_n = -\frac{1}{2}$
$y - 1 = -\frac{1}{2}(x - 1)$
$y = -\frac{1}{2}x + \frac{3}{2}$
b) Normal to $y = x^2$ at $x = 3$
Point: $(3, 9)$, tangent gradient: $m = 6$
Normal gradient: $m_n = -\frac{1}{6}$
$y - 9 = -\frac{1}{6}(x - 3)$
$y = -\frac{1}{6}x + \frac{19}{2}$
c) Normal to $y = x^2$ at $x = -1$
Point: $(-1, 1)$, tangent gradient: $m = -2$
Normal gradient: $m_n = \frac{1}{2}$
$y - 1 = \frac{1}{2}(x + 1)$
$y = \frac{1}{2}x + \frac{3}{2}$

Set 4: Mixed practice

a) Tangent to $y = 2x^2 + 3$ at $x = 1$
Point: $(1, 5)$
$\frac{dy}{dx} = 4x$, at $x = 1$: $m = 4$
$y - 5 = 4(x - 1)$
$y = 4x + 1$
b) Normal to $y = x^3 - x$ at $x = 2$
Point: $(2, 6)$
$\frac{dy}{dx} = 3x^2 - 1$, at $x = 2$: $m_t = 11$
Normal gradient: $m_n = -\frac{1}{11}$
$y - 6 = -\frac{1}{11}(x - 2)$
$y = -\frac{1}{11}x + \frac{68}{11}$
c) Tangent to $y = (x + 2)^2$ at $x = -1$
Point: $(-1, 1)$
$\frac{dy}{dx} = 2(x + 2)$, at $x = -1$: $m = 2$
$y - 1 = 2(x + 1)$
$y = 2x + 3$

Key Points to Remember

  • ✓ Always find the point first $(x_1, y_1)$
  • ✓ Tangent gradient = value of $\frac{dy}{dx}$ at the point
  • ✓ Normal gradient = $-\frac{1}{\text{tangent gradient}}$
  • ✓ Use point-gradient form: $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$
  • ✓ Tangent and normal are perpendicular: $m_1 \times m_2 = -1$
