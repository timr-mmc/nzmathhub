Connecting Rates of Change

Related rates problems involve finding how one quantity changes with respect to time, when we know how another related quantity changes with respect to time.

Chain Rule for Related Rates

If $y$ depends on $x$, and $x$ depends on $t$ (time), then:

$$\frac{dy}{dt} = \frac{dy}{dx} \times \frac{dx}{dt}$$

  1. Identify variables: What changes with time?
  2. Find the relationship: Connect variables with an equation
  3. Differentiate: Apply chain rule
  4. Substitute values: Use given information

Understanding the Chain

Rate of change of $y$ with time = Rate of change of $y$ with $x$ × Rate of change of $x$ with time

Think: "How fast is $y$ changing?" depends on "How fast is $x$ changing?" and "How sensitive is $y$ to changes in $x$?"

Worked Examples

Example 1: Expanding Circle

Question: The radius of a circle increases at 2 cm/s. Find the rate of increase of the area when $r = 5$ cm.
Step 1: Given information ▼
$\frac{dr}{dt} = 2$ cm/s, $r = 5$ cm
Step 2: Relationship ▼
$A = \pi r^2$
Step 3: Differentiate with respect to time ▼
$\frac{dA}{dt} = \frac{dA}{dr} \times \frac{dr}{dt}$
Step 4: Find $\frac{dA}{dr}$ ▼
$\frac{dA}{dr} = 2\pi r$
✓ Answer: Substitute ▼
$\frac{dA}{dt} = 2\pi r \times 2 = 4\pi r$
At $r = 5$: $\frac{dA}{dt} = 4\pi(5) = 20\pi$ cm²/s $\approx 62.8$ cm²/s

Example 2: Expanding Cube

Question: Each edge of a cube increases at 0.5 cm/s. Find the rate of increase of volume when edge length is 4 cm.
Step 1: Given ▼
$\frac{dx}{dt} = 0.5$ cm/s, $x = 4$ cm (edge length)
Step 2: Relationship ▼
$V = x^3$
Step 3: Chain rule ▼
$\frac{dV}{dt} = \frac{dV}{dx} \times \frac{dx}{dt}$
Step 4: Differentiate ▼
$\frac{dV}{dx} = 3x^2$
✓ Answer: Calculate ▼
$\frac{dV}{dt} = 3x^2 \times 0.5 = 1.5x^2$
At $x = 4$: $\frac{dV}{dt} = 1.5(16) = 24$ cm³/s

Example 3: Water Draining from Tank

Question: Water drains from a spherical tank. When radius of water surface is 3 m, it's decreasing at 0.1 m/s. Find rate of volume decrease. ($V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$)
Step 1: Given ▼
$\frac{dr}{dt} = -0.1$ m/s (negative because decreasing), $r = 3$ m
Step 2: Relationship ▼
$V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$
Step 3: Differentiate ▼
$\frac{dV}{dr} = \frac{4}{3}\pi \cdot 3r^2 = 4\pi r^2$
Step 4: Apply chain rule ▼
$\frac{dV}{dt} = 4\pi r^2 \times \frac{dr}{dt}$
✓ Answer: Calculate ▼
$\frac{dV}{dt} = 4\pi(3)^2 \times (-0.1) = -3.6\pi$ m³/s
Volume decreasing at $3.6\pi \approx 11.3$ m³/s

Example 4: Ladder Sliding Down Wall

Question: A 5 m ladder leans against a wall. The bottom slides away at 0.5 m/s. How fast is the top sliding down when the bottom is 3 m from the wall?
Step 1: Variables ▼
$x$ = distance from wall (bottom), $y$ = height up wall (top)
Given: $\frac{dx}{dt} = 0.5$ m/s, $x = 3$ m, ladder length = 5 m
Step 2: Relationship (Pythagoras) ▼
$x^2 + y^2 = 25$
Step 3: Find $y$ when $x = 3$ ▼
$9 + y^2 = 25$, so $y = 4$ m
Step 4: Differentiate with respect to $t$ ▼
$2x\frac{dx}{dt} + 2y\frac{dy}{dt} = 0$
✓ Answer: Solve for $\frac{dy}{dt}$ ▼
$2(3)(0.5) + 2(4)\frac{dy}{dt} = 0$
$3 + 8\frac{dy}{dt} = 0$
$\frac{dy}{dt} = -\frac{3}{8} = -0.375$ m/s
Top sliding down at 0.375 m/s

Practice: Minimal Change

Set 1: Circle - varying the rate

Radius increases at different rates, find $\frac{dA}{dt}$ when $r = 10$ cm

a) $\frac{dr}{dt} = 1$ cm/s, $r = 10$ cm. Find $\frac{dA}{dt}$
$A = \pi r^2$, so $\frac{dA}{dr} = 2\pi r$
$\frac{dA}{dt} = 2\pi r \times \frac{dr}{dt} = 2\pi(10)(1) = 20\pi$ cm²/s
b) $\frac{dr}{dt} = 2$ cm/s, $r = 10$ cm. Find $\frac{dA}{dt}$
$\frac{dA}{dt} = 2\pi r \times \frac{dr}{dt} = 2\pi(10)(2) = 40\pi$ cm²/s
c) $\frac{dr}{dt} = 3$ cm/s, $r = 10$ cm. Find $\frac{dA}{dt}$
$\frac{dA}{dt} = 2\pi r \times \frac{dr}{dt} = 2\pi(10)(3) = 60\pi$ cm²/s

Set 2: Circle - varying the radius

Radius increases at 2 cm/s, find $\frac{dA}{dt}$ at different radii

a) $\frac{dr}{dt} = 2$ cm/s, $r = 5$ cm. Find $\frac{dA}{dt}$
$\frac{dA}{dt} = 2\pi r \times 2 = 4\pi(5) = 20\pi$ cm²/s
b) $\frac{dr}{dt} = 2$ cm/s, $r = 10$ cm. Find $\frac{dA}{dt}$
$\frac{dA}{dt} = 4\pi(10) = 40\pi$ cm²/s
c) $\frac{dr}{dt} = 2$ cm/s, $r = 15$ cm. Find $\frac{dA}{dt}$
$\frac{dA}{dt} = 4\pi(15) = 60\pi$ cm²/s

Set 3: Cube - varying dimensions

Edge increases at 1 cm/s, find $\frac{dV}{dt}$ at different sizes

a) $\frac{dx}{dt} = 1$ cm/s, $x = 2$ cm. Find $\frac{dV}{dt}$
$V = x^3$, so $\frac{dV}{dx} = 3x^2$
$\frac{dV}{dt} = 3x^2 \times 1 = 3(2)^2 = 12$ cm³/s
b) $\frac{dx}{dt} = 1$ cm/s, $x = 3$ cm. Find $\frac{dV}{dt}$
$\frac{dV}{dt} = 3(3)^2 = 27$ cm³/s
c) $\frac{dx}{dt} = 1$ cm/s, $x = 5$ cm. Find $\frac{dV}{dt}$
$\frac{dV}{dt} = 3(5)^2 = 75$ cm³/s

Set 4: Application problems

a) A square's side increases at 3 cm/s. Find rate of area increase when side is 4 cm.
$A = x^2$, so $\frac{dA}{dx} = 2x$
$\frac{dA}{dt} = 2x \times 3 = 2(4)(3) = 24$ cm²/s
b) Surface area of sphere: $S = 4\pi r^2$. Find $\frac{dS}{dt}$ when $r = 6$ cm and $\frac{dr}{dt} = 0.5$ cm/s.
$\frac{dS}{dr} = 8\pi r$
$\frac{dS}{dt} = 8\pi r \times 0.5 = 4\pi(6) = 24\pi$ cm²/s
c) A 10 m ladder against wall. Bottom slides at 0.4 m/s. Find $\frac{dy}{dt}$ when $x = 6$ m.
$x^2 + y^2 = 100$. When $x = 6$: $y = 8$
$2x\frac{dx}{dt} + 2y\frac{dy}{dt} = 0$
$2(6)(0.4) + 2(8)\frac{dy}{dt} = 0$
$\frac{dy}{dt} = -\frac{4.8}{16} = -0.3$ m/s

Key Points to Remember

  • ✓ Chain rule is key: $\frac{dy}{dt} = \frac{dy}{dx} \times \frac{dx}{dt}$
  • ✓ Identify what's changing and what you need to find
  • ✓ Find the relationship between variables (formula)
  • ✓ Differentiate with respect to time using chain rule
  • ✓ Watch for negative rates (decreasing quantities)
  • ✓ Units matter: check your answer makes sense
