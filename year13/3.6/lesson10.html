Complex Worked Examples

Example 1: Combining Product & Chain Rules with Tangents

Question: Find the equation of the tangent to $y = x^2(2x + 1)^3$ at $x = 1$
Step 1: Find the point ▼
$y = 1^2(3)^3 = 27$, so point is $(1, 27)$
Step 2: Differentiate (product rule) ▼
$u = x^2, v = (2x + 1)^3$
$u' = 2x$
$v' = 3(2x + 1)^2 \times 2 = 6(2x + 1)^2$ (chain rule)
Step 3: Apply product rule ▼
$\frac{dy}{dx} = x^2 \cdot 6(2x + 1)^2 + (2x + 1)^3 \cdot 2x$
$= 2x(2x + 1)^2[3x + (2x + 1)]$
$= 2x(2x + 1)^2(5x + 1)$
Step 4: Evaluate at $x = 1$ ▼
$m = 2(1)(3)^2(6) = 108$
✓ Tangent equation ▼
$y - 27 = 108(x - 1)$
$y = 108x - 81$

Example 2: Optimisation with Multiple Constraints

Question: A cylindrical can must hold 500 cm³. The top and bottom cost $2/cm², the side costs $1/cm². Find dimensions to minimise cost.
Step 1: Variables ▼
Radius $r$, height $h$. Volume: $\pi r^2 h = 500$
Step 2: Cost function ▼
Top + Bottom: $2(\pi r^2) \times 2 = 4\pi r^2$
Side: $(2\pi rh) \times 1 = 2\pi rh$
Total: $C = 4\pi r^2 + 2\pi rh$
Step 3: Eliminate h ▼
$h = \frac{500}{\pi r^2}$
$C = 4\pi r^2 + 2\pi r \cdot \frac{500}{\pi r^2} = 4\pi r^2 + \frac{1000}{r}$
Step 4: Differentiate ▼
$\frac{dC}{dr} = 8\pi r - \frac{1000}{r^2}$
Step 5: Solve ▼
$8\pi r - \frac{1000}{r^2} = 0$
$8\pi r^3 = 1000$
$r^3 = \frac{1000}{8\pi} = \frac{125}{\pi}$
$r = \sqrt[3]{\frac{125}{\pi}} \approx 3.41$ cm
Step 6: Find h ▼
$h = \frac{500}{\pi(3.41)^2} \approx 13.7$ cm
✓ Verify minimum ▼
$\frac{d^2C}{dr^2} = 8\pi + \frac{2000}{r^3} > 0$ ✓

Example 3: Related Rates with Multiple Variables

Question: A cone-shaped tank (vertex down) has height 10 m and radius 4 m. Water flows in at 2 m³/min. How fast is the water level rising when depth is 6 m?
Step 1: Given ▼
$\frac{dV}{dt} = 2$ m³/min, find $\frac{dh}{dt}$ when $h = 6$ m
Step 2: Volume formula ▼
$V = \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h$
Step 3: Find relationship between r and h ▼
By similar triangles: $\frac{r}{h} = \frac{4}{10}$, so $r = \frac{2h}{5}$
Step 4: Substitute ▼
$V = \frac{1}{3}\pi(\frac{2h}{5})^2h = \frac{4\pi h^3}{75}$
Step 5: Differentiate with respect to time ▼
$\frac{dV}{dt} = \frac{4\pi}{75} \times 3h^2 \times \frac{dh}{dt} = \frac{4\pi h^2}{25} \times \frac{dh}{dt}$
✓ Solve for $\frac{dh}{dt}$ ▼
$2 = \frac{4\pi(36)}{25} \times \frac{dh}{dt}$
$\frac{dh}{dt} = \frac{50}{144\pi} = \frac{25}{72\pi} \approx 0.11$ m/min

Example 4: Maximum Area with Geometric Constraints

Question: A Norman window consists of a rectangle topped by a semicircle. The perimeter is 12 m. Find dimensions for maximum light (area).
Step 1: Variables ▼
Width $x$ (diameter of semicircle), height of rectangle $h$
Step 2: Perimeter ▼
$x + 2h + \pi(\frac{x}{2}) = 12$
$x + 2h + \frac{\pi x}{2} = 12$
$h = \frac{12 - x(1 + \frac{\pi}{2})}{2} = 6 - x(\frac{2 + \pi}{4})$
Step 3: Area ▼
Rectangle + Semicircle
$A = xh + \frac{\pi}{2}(\frac{x}{2})^2 = xh + \frac{\pi x^2}{8}$
Step 4: Substitute for h ▼
$A = x[6 - x(\frac{2 + \pi}{4})] + \frac{\pi x^2}{8}$
$= 6x - \frac{x^2(2 + \pi)}{4} + \frac{\pi x^2}{8}$
$= 6x - \frac{2x^2}{4} - \frac{\pi x^2}{4} + \frac{\pi x^2}{8}$
$= 6x - \frac{x^2}{2} - \frac{\pi x^2}{8} = 6x - x^2(\frac{4 + \pi}{8})$
Step 5: Differentiate ▼
$\frac{dA}{dx} = 6 - x(\frac{4 + \pi}{4})$
✓ Solve ▼
$6 = x(\frac{4 + \pi}{4})$
$x = \frac{24}{4 + \pi} \approx 3.36$ m
$h = 6 - 3.36(\frac{2 + \pi}{4}) \approx 1.68$ m

Challenge Problems

Test Your Complete Understanding

1. A particle moves along the curve $y = x^3 - 12x + 5$. Find where the particle is moving horizontally.
Horizontal movement means $\frac{dy}{dx} = 0$
$\frac{dy}{dx} = 3x^2 - 12 = 0$
$x^2 = 4$, so $x = \pm 2$
At $x = 2$: $y = 8 - 24 + 5 = -11$
At $x = -2$: $y = -8 + 24 + 5 = 21$
Points: $(2, -11)$ and $(-2, 21)$
2. Find tangent to $y = \frac{x}{(x + 1)^2}$ at $x = 0$.
Point: $(0, 0)$
Using quotient rule: $u = x, u' = 1; v = (x + 1)^2, v' = 2(x + 1)$
$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{(x + 1)^2 \cdot 1 - x \cdot 2(x + 1)}{(x + 1)^4}$
$= \frac{(x + 1)[(x + 1) - 2x]}{(x + 1)^4} = \frac{1 - x}{(x + 1)^3}$
At $x = 0$: $m = 1$
Tangent: $y = x$
3. A rectangular poster has area 300 cm². It has margins: 4 cm top/bottom, 2 cm left/right. Maximise printed area.
Poster: width $x$, height $y$. $xy = 300$
Printed area: $A = (x - 4)(y - 8)$
Substitute $y = \frac{300}{x}$:
$A = (x - 4)(\frac{300}{x} - 8) = 300 - 8x - \frac{1200}{x} + 32 = 332 - 8x - \frac{1200}{x}$
$\frac{dA}{dx} = -8 + \frac{1200}{x^2} = 0$
$x^2 = 150$, $x = \sqrt{150} \approx 12.25$ cm
$y = \frac{300}{12.25} \approx 24.5$ cm
4. Find stationary points and points of inflection for $y = x^4 - 4x^3$.
$\frac{dy}{dx} = 4x^3 - 12x^2 = 4x^2(x - 3) = 0$
Stationary points: $x = 0, x = 3$
At $x = 0$: $y = 0$; At $x = 3$: $y = 81 - 108 = -27$
$\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = 12x^2 - 24x = 12x(x - 2)$
At $x = 0$: $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = 0$ (test further - horizontal inflection)
At $x = 3$: $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = 36 > 0$ → minimum
Inflection: $12x(x - 2) = 0 \Rightarrow x = 0$ or $x = 2$
$(0, 0)$ horizontal inflection, $(2, -16)$ non-horizontal inflection

Complete Differentiation Summary

Rules

  • Power: $(x^n)' = nx^{n-1}$
  • Product: $(uv)' = uv' + vu'$
  • Quotient: $(\frac{u}{v})' = \frac{vu' - uv'}{v^2}$
  • Chain: $\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy}{du} \cdot \frac{du}{dx}$

Tangents & Normals

  • Tangent: $m = \frac{dy}{dx}$ at point
  • Normal: $m = -\frac{1}{\frac{dy}{dx}}$
  • Equation: $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$

Stationary Points

  • Occur where $\frac{dy}{dx} = 0$
  • Max: $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} < 0$
  • Min: $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} > 0$
  • Inflection: sign change in $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}$

Applications

  • Related rates: Chain rule with time
  • Optimisation: Find max/min values
  • Use constraints to reduce variables
  • Always verify with 2nd derivative
