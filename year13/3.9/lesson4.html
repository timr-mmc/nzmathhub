Linear Regression

#
Definition: Linear regression finds the "line of best fit" that best represents the relationship between two variables. This line minimizes the sum of squared vertical distances from data points to the line.

Why Use a Line of Best Fit?

  • Summarize the relationship between variables
  • Make predictions for new values
  • Quantify the relationship mathematically
  • Compare different datasets

Least Squares Method

The line of best fit is found using the least squares method, which minimizes the sum of squared residuals (vertical distances from points to the line).

💡 Key Point: The line always passes through the point $(\bar{x}, \bar{y})$ (the means of x and y).

Assumptions for Linear Regression

  1. The relationship between variables is approximately linear
  2. Points show relatively constant scatter around the line
  3. No influential outliers severely affecting the line
2

The Regression Equation

#

General Form

$$y = a + bx$$

Where:

  • $y$ = predicted value of response variable
  • $x$ = value of explanatory variable
  • $a$ = y-intercept (value of y when x = 0)
  • $b$ = slope (change in y for each unit increase in x)

Finding the Slope (b)

$$b = \frac{n\sum xy - \sum x \sum y}{n\sum x^2 - (\sum x)^2}$$

Finding the y-intercept (a)

$$a = \bar{y} - b\bar{x}$$

Where $\bar{x}$ and $\bar{y}$ are the means of x and y respectively.

Worked Example

Using the data from Lesson 3:

x y
25
48
611
813

We found: $n = 4$, $\sum x = 20$, $\sum y = 37$, $\sum x^2 = 120$, $\sum xy = 212$

Step 1: Calculate the slope (b)

$$b = \frac{4(212) - (20)(37)}{4(120) - 20^2} = \frac{848 - 740}{480 - 400} = \frac{108}{80} = 1.35$$

Step 2: Calculate means

$\bar{x} = \frac{20}{4} = 5$ and $\bar{y} = \frac{37}{4} = 9.25$

Step 3: Calculate y-intercept (a)

$$a = 9.25 - 1.35(5) = 9.25 - 6.75 = 2.5$$

Final Equation

$$y = 2.5 + 1.35x$$

Interpretation: For every 1-unit increase in x, y increases by 1.35 units on average. When x = 0, we predict y = 2.5.

3

Coefficient of Determination (R²)

#
Definition: R² (R-squared) measures the proportion of variation in y that is explained by the linear relationship with x.

Understanding R²

$$R^2 = r^2$$

R² is simply the square of the correlation coefficient.

Interpreting R²

  • Range: $0 \leq R^2 \leq 1$ (or 0% to 100%)
  • $R^2 = 0$: The line explains none of the variation in y
  • $R^2 = 1$: The line explains all of the variation in y
  • Expressed as a percentage: multiply by 100

Example

If $r = 0.9$, then $R^2 = 0.9^2 = 0.81$ or 81%

Interpretation: "81% of the variation in y can be explained by the linear relationship with x. The remaining 19% is due to other factors or random variation."

💡 Remember: Higher R² means the line fits the data better and predictions will be more accurate.

What R² Tells Us

R² Value Interpretation
$R^2 > 0.8$ Strong model - line fits data well
$0.5 < R^2 < 0.8$ Moderate model - some predictive power
$R^2 < 0.5$ Weak model - limited predictive power
