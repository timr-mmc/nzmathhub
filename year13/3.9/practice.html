Problem 1: Identifying Variables

A researcher is studying the relationship between hours of sleep and reaction time in milliseconds.

a) Identify the explanatory and response variables.

b) What type of relationship would you expect? Why?

Show Answer

a) Explanatory variable: Hours of sleep (x-axis)
Response variable: Reaction time (y-axis)

b) Negative relationship - as sleep increases, reaction time should decrease (faster reactions with more sleep).

Problem 2: Interpreting Correlation

The correlation between temperature and heating costs is r = -0.82.

a) Describe this relationship in words.

b) What percentage of variation in heating costs is explained by temperature?

Show Answer

a) There is a strong negative linear relationship between temperature and heating costs. As temperature increases, heating costs tend to decrease significantly.

b) R² = (-0.82)² = 0.6724 or 67.24%. About 67% of variation in heating costs can be explained by temperature.

Problem 3: Using a Regression Equation

The regression equation for predicting house price ($1000s) from size (m²) is:

Price = 50 + 2.5(Size)

a) Predict the price of a 120m² house.

b) What does the slope mean in context?

c) What does the y-intercept mean? Is it meaningful?

Show Answer

a) Price = 50 + 2.5(120) = 50 + 300 = $350,000

b) For each additional square meter, the house price increases by $2,500 on average.

c) When size = 0, price = $50,000. This isn't meaningful in context since a house can't have 0m², but it's needed for the mathematical model.

Problem 5: Interpolation vs Extrapolation

Data on car age (years) vs value ($1000s) was collected for cars aged 1-8 years.

Regression equation: Value = 25 - 2(Age)

a) Predict the value of a 5-year-old car. Is this reliable?

b) Predict the value of a 15-year-old car. Is this reliable?

c) Predict the value of a brand new car (age = 0). Is this meaningful?

Show Answer

a) Value = 25 - 2(5) = $15,000. This is interpolation (within 1-8 years range), so reasonably reliable.

b) Value = 25 - 2(15) = -$5,000. This is extrapolation and gives an impossible negative value! Not reliable - the linear model breaks down for old cars.

c) Value = 25 - 2(0) = $25,000. This is extrapolation but close to the data range. It suggests new cars are worth about $25,000, which might be reasonable but should be stated with caution.

Problem 6: Outlier Analysis

In a study of advertising spend vs sales, most data shows r = 0.75. One company spent $100,000 on advertising but had zero sales.

a) Is this point an outlier? Why?

b) Suggest possible explanations for this observation.

c) Should this point be kept in the analysis?

Show Answer

a) Yes, this is an outlier. It doesn't fit the positive relationship shown by other data - high advertising should lead to high sales, not zero.

b) Possible explanations:

  • Data entry error (maybe $1,000 not $100,000, or sales of $10,000 not $0)
  • New product that failed despite advertising
  • Advertising happened but product wasn't available for purchase
  • Different industry/product not comparable to others
  • Time lag - sales haven't happened yet

c) Investigate first! If it's an error, correct it. If genuine, consider reporting analyses both with and without it, explaining the difference. If it represents a completely different situation, it might be appropriate to remove it and note this in your report.

Problem 7: Comprehensive Analysis

A study investigated the relationship between daily screen time (hours) and sleep quality score (0-100) for 50 teenagers. Results:

  • r = -0.68
  • Regression equation: Quality = 85 - 4(ScreenTime)
  • Screen time ranged from 2 to 10 hours
  • One student with 9 hours screen time scored 95 on sleep quality

Write a comprehensive statistical report covering:

a) Description of the relationship (direction, form, strength)

b) Interpretation of the slope and y-intercept

c) R² and what it means

d) The unusual point and its potential effect

e) Predictions and their reliability

f) Limitations and conclusions

Show Model Answer

a) Relationship Description:

There is a moderate to strong negative linear relationship (r = -0.68) between daily screen time and sleep quality for teenagers. As screen time increases, sleep quality tends to decrease.

b) Equation Interpretation:

Slope: For each additional hour of screen time per day, sleep quality score decreases by 4 points on average.

Y-intercept: The model predicts a sleep quality score of 85 when screen time is zero. This represents the baseline sleep quality without screen time.

c) Coefficient of Determination:

R² = (-0.68)² = 0.46 or 46%. This means 46% of the variation in sleep quality can be explained by screen time. The remaining 54% is due to other factors (diet, exercise, stress, etc.).

d) Outlier Analysis:

The student with 9 hours screen time but quality score of 95 is a significant outlier. The model predicts: Quality = 85 - 4(9) = 49, but actual is 95 (difference of 46 points). This student may have exceptional sleep habits, be less sensitive to screens, or there may be a data recording error. This point likely weakens the correlation - without it, r might be stronger. This should be investigated and potentially analyzed separately.

e) Predictions:

For 5 hours screen time: Quality = 85 - 4(5) = 65. This is interpolation within the observed range (2-10 hours) and is reasonably reliable given the moderate strength of the relationship.

For 15 hours screen time: Quality = 85 - 4(15) = 25. This is extrapolation beyond the observed range and should be treated cautiously - the linear relationship may not hold at extreme screen time levels.

f) Limitations and Conclusions:

The study shows a clear negative relationship between screen time and sleep quality in teenagers, with screen time explaining nearly half the variation in sleep quality. However, this is observational data - we cannot conclude that screen time causes poor sleep quality (correlation ≠ causation). Other factors may be involved, and the presence of an influential outlier suggests the relationship may not be consistent across all individuals. Predictions should be limited to the 2-10 hour range observed in the data. Further research could investigate the outlier and other factors affecting sleep quality.

💡 Exam Tip: Always structure your answers using DFSU (Direction, Form, Strength, Unusual features) and remember to write in context - use the actual variable names, not just "x" and "y"!
