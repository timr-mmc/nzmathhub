What is a Comparison Investigation?

#
Focus: A comparison investigation involves comparing two or more groups or populations to identify differences and similarities in a variable of interest.

Key Features

  • Compares groups or categories
  • Uses measures of center and spread
  • Employs visual displays (box plots, histograms, dot plots)
  • Considers sample size and variability
  • Makes evidence-based conclusions

Example Questions

  • Do Year 11 students spend more time on homework than Year 10 students?
  • Is there a difference in reaction times between males and females?
  • Do athletes have lower resting heart rates than non-athletes?
  • Which teaching method leads to better test scores?
